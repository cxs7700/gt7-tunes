Braking requires a bit of finesse. Under heavy braking, avoid applying too much steering input—doing so can cause the rear to step out. While this slide is usually easy to catch thanks to the car's stability, it’s not the fastest way through the corner. A better approach is to brake a bit earlier and modulate your braking pressure, focusing on keeping front-end grip and hitting the ideal line into the apex. Smooth inputs will reward you with consistent lap times and better tire wear over longer stints. This setup also works well in a max-power configuration, using a high-rpm turbo and maximum ECU output.
