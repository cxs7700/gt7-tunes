Physics Simulation Model - The behavioral model for suspension systems has been adjusted.  ・Reduced sudden changes in behavior when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.This is a great improvement. When going through strong compressions, like Fox Hole at Nordschleife, the car remains stable—you won’t experience for some cars a sudden weight balance shifts or losing the driving line. - The tire physics simulation model has been adjusted.  ・Adjustments have been made on load dependency while cornering.  ・Grip loss on racing lines on wet surfaces has been reduced.  ・The impact of dirty tires from off-track excursions has been reduced, with faster recovery of grip when returning to the track.
This is exactly what I had been asking PD for a long time—less sudden loss of grip when exceeding the optimal traction, whether laterally or longitudinally under acceleration, braking, or cornering.
Previously, grip would suddenly drop from 100% to 50%, but now it feels more like 100% to 75%. This results in a more progressive loss of grip, making the car easier to control, providing better feedback, and enhancing driving pleasure.
Additionally, off-track surfaces—such as grass or the green zones beyond the curbs—now offer more grip. - Updates have been made to the handling of some cars.  ・Adjustments have been made to default aerodynamic settings for Gr.1 cars and some race cars.  ・Adjustments have been made to the default suspension and differential settings for some road cars. - Performance Points (PP) for some cars have been updated. - The effects of the following driving assists have been updated:  ・The Traction Control (TCS) assist intervention has been optimized.  ・Corner braking control for ABS (anti-lock brakes) has been optimized.
It looks like they’ve fixed some broken cars… let’s see.
The Garage RCR Civic seems more drivable now—the updated grip loss physics have significantly reduced front wheel spin, making it much more manageable.Most pre-1.54 PP setups need some adjustments.
Traction control has been optimized, and the tire physics likely play a big role in this new behavior, along with the updated TC system.
