ENGINE SWAP : 8.0-WR16-64v4T-Chiron (Bugatti Chiron)
Wide Car.
Rims 20" & 21 - rims width wide - Offset wide
Front B - Side St - Rear A - Wing A
This is the full downforce version of the build. The previous setup used the original wing and spoiler. To compare: on Daytona Road, both versions lap around 1:32. But on more technical tracks like Spa or the Nordschleife, the increased grip gives this setup a slight edge in performance.
I’ve used Racing Soft tires to maximize grip on tight, technical sections, but you can switch to Mediums if you want to unlock more power. Even on Mediums, you’ll still get solid grip, and there’s a lot of untapped horsepower in this build. The Chiron engine, when fully maxed, can hit an insane 1850 HP and 1520 ft-lb of torque — this is a seriously wild swap.
Performance Notes
In terms of pace, it’s right up there with the top 800PP cars. Extremely grippy and stable. I added some rear ballast to shift the balance slightly — running 49/51 (front/rear) instead of the original 52/48. That slight change helps the front end feel lighter and more responsive on corner entry. The stock balance felt a bit sluggish to me.
