Standing start: Set 1st gear for an optimal launch. Focus on maximum acceleration off the line without excessive wheelspin.Rolling start: Set 1st gear based on the slowest corner exit on the track. Aim for optimal traction and a safe, controlled exit speed.
Step 3: Tune Final GearSet the top gear for the fastest point on the circuit (e.g., longest straight).
Add a margin for extra speed during drafting.Ensure you reach this top speed at the optimal RPM where maximum power is delivered (check the power/torque graph or first picture for peak values).
Step 4: Distribute Intermediate Gears
Start by spreading the intermediate gears evenly between 1st and final gear. This provides smooth progression and balanced acceleration.Fine-tune for specific corners:Sometimes, adjust a gear for better front grip on corner entry (via downshift RPM).Other times, optimize a gear for exit traction and control.
