Gearbox Tuning in GT7: Key PrinciplesGearbox settings in Gran Turismo 7 (GT7) don't make a huge difference overall—they're mainly beneficial for peaky engines (those with narrow power bands).Step 1: Choose Your Start TypeFirst, decide if your setup is for a standing start (e.g., drag races) or a rolling start (e.g., most circuit races).Step 2: Tune 1st Gear Standing start: Set 1st gear for an optimal launch. Focus on maximum acceleration off the line without excessive wheelspin.Rolling start: Set 1st gear based on the slowest corner exit on the track. Aim for optimal traction and a safe, controlled exit speed. Step 3: Tune Final GearSet the top gear for the fastest point on the circuit (e.g., longest straight). Add a margin for extra speed during drafting.Ensure you reach this top speed at the optimal RPM where maximum power is delivered (check the power/torque graph or first picture for peak values). Step 4: Distribute Intermediate Gears Start by spreading the intermediate gears evenly between 1st and final gear. This provides smooth progression and balanced acceleration.Fine-tune for specific corners:Sometimes, adjust a gear for better front grip on corner entry (via downshift RPM).Other times, optimize a gear for exit traction and control. This approach keeps your gearbox versatile while maximizing performance where it counts. Test iteratively on the track!
