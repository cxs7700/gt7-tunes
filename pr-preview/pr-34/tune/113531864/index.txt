In my opinion, this is the best car to learn proper driving techniques in GT. It’s stable and responsive without being overly reactive, which makes it easy to understand how driving inputs work in a controlled manner , almost like driving in slow motion. You have the time to establish your racing line, modulate your speed without over-braking, and practice clean, fast corner exits.
It’s an excellent machine for the Lightweight Cup. While not an easy win—since the competition has similar performance, it’s also ideal for lobby racing at 400PP on the Nordschleife. The gearbox is tuned for the Nordschleife, but you can add 200 kg of ballast to improve top speed on faster tracks. On twistier layouts with long straights, performance remains consistent and reliable.
The Daihatsu Copen is slightly faster, with about 8 km/h more top speed, but the Honda offers better grip and sharper handling, making it the more enjoyable choice for technical driving.
