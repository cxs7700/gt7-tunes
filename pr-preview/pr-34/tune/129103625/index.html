I had to make the car heavier than I wanted because reducing the weight too much lowers the power too severely for the 700PP level. This setup is the best compromise I’ve found , it’s almost on pace with the Porsche 911 '16, which is always a solid benchmark. At Le Mans, fuel consumption is high. It’s difficult to manage 3-lap stints, even when using fuel map 6 and short shifting. For fans of that event, you might consider reducing the weight further by applying more weight reduction stages, then lowering the power to improve fuel efficiency.
